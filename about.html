Davyd Zakharniev

Film critic. Screenwriter. London.

Davyd Zakharniev writes about film. Specifically, about the gap between what a screenplay promises and what ends up on screen.

He was born in Kharkiv, Ukraine. He wrote prose and poetry before he ever touched a camera. The writing came first - short fiction, verse, fragments in notebooks - and it still comes first. Cinema found him later, but it stuck, because film is the only medium where language, image, and time collide in the same frame.

He studies BA Film & Television at London Metropolitan University. His influences are wide: David Fincher's control, Tarkovsky's patience, Nolan's nerve. But the writers interest him most - Charlie Kaufman's recursions, the Coen Brothers' ear for silence, Aaron Sorkin's belief that dialogue is a contact sport.

davydwrites is where he reviews new releases, dissects screenplays, and argues about whether the book was actually better. It updates weekly. He is always watching something.
