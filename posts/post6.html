Why I started writing about film (and why I will not stop)

I did not grow up wanting to be a film critic. I grew up in Kharkiv wanting to make things. Stories - in notebooks that nobody would ever read. Prose, poetry, fragments of something I could not name yet. Cinema found me later. It stuck because film does something writing alone cannot do: it makes time visible. A cut is an argument. A held shot is a demand.

This blog exists because I needed somewhere to be honest about the films I watch. Not polite. Not academic. Not the kind of writing where every opinion comes with three qualifications and a "to be fair." I wanted to write the kind of criticism I actually want to read - specific, blunt, interested in craft, unimpressed by budget.

I am a student at London Metropolitan University. BA Film and Television. I am still learning how films work. I will be wrong about things - probably often. But I would rather be wrong and specific than right and vague. Vague is the enemy. Vague is what happens when you are afraid someone might disagree with you.

If you have read this far - thanks. New posts every week. I am always watching something.
